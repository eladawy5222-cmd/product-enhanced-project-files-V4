# tourism business context

Use this reference when defining the offer, audience, positioning, product catalog, or business model for a tourism company or domestic tour operator.

## core tourism offer fields

For any trip, product, package, or landing page, try to capture or infer:

- destination and starting city
- trip type: day tour, weekend trip, group tour, private tour, family trip, honeymoon, adventure, cultural, beach, luxury, budget, corporate, school/university
- duration and schedule
- available dates or season
- price or price range
- inclusions: transport, guide, hotel, meals, tickets, activities, insurance, support
- exclusions: personal expenses, optional activities, taxes, flights, tips, visa, permits
- itinerary by day or by hour
- pickup/drop-off points
- target traveler and pain points
- cancellation, refund, and rescheduling rules
- booking method and payment method
- proof/trust: reviews, photos, license, guide credentials, years of experience, partners

## useful traveler personas

1. **families**: safety, comfort, clean transport, child-friendly stops, clear schedule, predictable costs.
2. **couples**: privacy, romantic moments, photography, premium hotel options, relaxed pace.
3. **young groups**: price, fun, activities, social proof, instagrammable places, flexible payment.
4. **corporate groups**: punctuality, invoices, team-building, risk control, professional coordination.
5. **schools/universities**: safety, supervision, educational value, approvals, group management.
6. **luxury travelers**: exclusivity, private transfers, boutique stays, concierge support, customization.
7. **budget travelers**: clear value, transparent inclusions, group pricing, installment options.
8. **international travelers**: language support, airport transfer, local expertise, cultural context, safety.

## positioning angles

Choose one primary angle per page or campaign:

- best value domestic tours
- private customized trips
- family-safe travel company
- expert local guides
- fast whatsapp booking
- premium comfort and trusted service
- hidden gems and authentic local experiences
- seasonal offer or holiday package
- corporate/team travel partner

## tourism product design checklist

- Make the itinerary easy to imagine and easy to compare.
- State what is included and excluded clearly to reduce objections.
- Offer optional upgrades where possible: private car, hotel category, meal plan, photographer, guide language.
- Add urgency only when real: limited seats, confirmed departure, holiday demand, early booking deadline.
- Provide backup plans for weather, traffic, sold-out attractions, or seasonal closures.
- Include a clear next action: ask for availability, reserve seat, request full itinerary, speak to consultant.

## assumptions to state when data is missing

When the user has not provided enough details, use a short assumptions block such as:

- assumed business type: domestic tour company
- assumed target audience: families and young groups
- assumed main conversion channel: whatsapp leads
- assumed website goal: generate booking inquiries
- assumed language: arabic primary with english support

Then continue with a practical deliverable instead of stopping.
