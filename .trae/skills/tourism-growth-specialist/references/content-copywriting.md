# tourism content and copywriting playbook

Use this reference for articles, trip descriptions, program descriptions, social posts, ads, emails, bilingual content, and conversion copy.

## voice and tone

- helpful, confident, warm, and practical.
- inspiring but specific: show the experience, not vague excitement.
- trustworthy: make inclusions, limitations, and next steps clear.
- locally aware: adapt examples, currency, culture, holidays, and travel habits to the target market.
- bilingual quality: write arabic and english as native marketing copy, not word-for-word translation.

## tour description formula

1. opening promise: what the traveler will feel or experience.
2. practical summary: destination, duration, style, and who it suits.
3. highlights: 3-6 concrete attractions or experiences.
4. itinerary: logical order with time/day if known.
5. inclusions/exclusions.
6. why book with this company.
7. faq or notes.
8. cta.

## article formula for tourism seo

- title aligned with search intent.
- short intro that answers the main query quickly.
- table of contents for longer articles.
- practical sections: best time, how to get there, itinerary, cost factors, what to pack, safety, faq.
- internal links to relevant tour pages.
- cta that fits the article intent, not a hard sell too early.
- update note when seasonality or prices matter.

## ad copy angles

Use different angles depending on audience and channel:

- escape routine: weekend trip, quick refresh, easy booking.
- family comfort: safe transport, clear program, support.
- social proof: trusted by travelers, real reviews, popular trip.
- urgency: confirmed departure or limited seats when true.
- value: inclusions, transparent pricing, group offer.
- customization: private trip built around your date and budget.
- seasonality: holiday, eid, summer, winter desert, long weekend.

## social campaign structure

For a campaign, provide:

- campaign objective
- target audience
- main offer
- content pillars
- post ideas
- captions
- creative direction
- cta
- hashtags when useful
- landing page or whatsapp flow
- measurement metrics

## email sequence structure

For tourism leads:

1. instant response: confirm inquiry, summarize requested trip, ask one key question.
2. value email: highlights, itinerary, trust signals.
3. objection email: price, safety, inclusions, dates, payment.
4. urgency/follow-up: availability, deadline, limited seats if true.
5. post-trip/re-engagement: review request, referral, next destination.

## arabic copy guidance

- Prefer clear modern arabic suitable for the target country.
- Use emotional phrases carefully; avoid exaggerated promises.
- Keep ctas direct: احجز الآن، اسأل عن المتاح، كلّم مستشار رحلات، اطلب البرنامج الكامل.
- Use colloquial arabic only when the brand voice is casual or social-first.
- For professional pages, use polished arabic with light friendly phrasing.

## english copy guidance

- Use concise tourism phrases: explore, discover, enjoy, hassle-free, guided, private, family-friendly, flexible, curated.
- Avoid clichés unless supported by specific details.
- Keep headlines benefit-led and simple.

## content verification rules

Before publishing, verify any mention of:

- prices, discounts, taxes, and payment terms
- dates, departure times, and availability
- hotel names, room types, and ratings
- visa, permits, safety, health, legal, or insurance rules
- attraction opening hours and seasonal closures
- transport duration and pickup points
